Dominican Spaghetti

Authentic Dominican Spaghetti covered in a homemade traditional Dominican tomato sauce that’s creamy, tasty, and out of this world!

Dominican Spaghetti is a traditional Dominican pasta dish that is truly out of this world! I’m not exaggerating when I say that this pasta recipe is one of my top 3 favorite Dominican recipes. This pasta dish is full of traditional and authentic Dominican flavors that sets it apart from Italian spaghetti.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (1 Box) Spaghetti Pasta
  • 1 Red or White Onion Minced
  • ½ Green Bell Pepper Minced
  • 2 Garlic Cloves Minced
  • 1 tbsp Chicken Bouillon Cube
  • 1 tbsp Sazón con Achiote
  • Black Pepper to taste
  • 1 cup Tomato Paste
  • 1 cup Tomato Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • ¼ cup Evaporated Milk
  • 2 tbsp Spanish Olives
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1 ½ cup Starchy Water
  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil

Steps

  1. Mince red or white onion, green bell pepper, and garlic cloves. Set aside
  2. Bring water to boil for pasta and add salt. Once the water is boiling, add pasta and boil al dente according to package instructions. Set aside 1 cup of starchy water. Drain pasta and shock with cold water. Set aside
  3. Set flame to medium. In a large braiser or caldero, add olive oil. Then add minced onions, peppers, and garlic. Saute for 3 minutes making sure to constantly mix. You do not want veggies to burn. Next, add chicken bouillon cube, Dominican oregano, black pepper, and sazon con achiote. Saute for another 2 minutes. Then add tomato paste and tomato sauce. Mix together until well combined. Afterwards, add starchy pasta water and continue to work into the sauce until tomato paste has been fully incorporated. Let sauce simmer for 2 minutes
  4. As the sauce simmer, taste and adjust for salt. Add Spanish olives and mix until well combined. Now add evaporated milk and continue to mix until well combined. Feel free to add extra milk if you want it creamier or water to help thin out the sauce. Add sugar and butter and mix
  5. Once the butter has melted, reduce flame to low. Carefully fold in spaghetti. Continue to mix in spaghetti until is well combined and covered in sauce. Turn off flame and let spaghetti sit covered for 2 minutes before serving. Enjoy with yuca, plantains, or your favorite side dish!
