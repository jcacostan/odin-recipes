Mangú

Traditional Dominican Mangú Recipe Made with Mashed Green Plantains

Dominican Mangu is a dish that’s very near and dear to my heart. Growing up I would wake up on Saturdays to the smell of boiling green plantains, sauteed onions, fried salami, cheese, and eggs. It’s a core childhood memory that instantly reminds me of my grandmother.

Ingredients

  • 4 Green Plantains Peeled cut in half or quarters
  • 2-3 tbs Salted Butter *or to taste
  • 2 Red Onions
  • ¼ cup White Distilled Vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Vegetable Oil
  • 1/2 tbsp Kosher Salt (for onions)
  • 2 tbsp Kosher Salt (for plantains)
  • 8 cups Water

Steps

  1. Cut red onions into rings then add white vinegar with 1/2 tbsp kosher salt. Set aside and let onions subtly pickle
  2. Peel green plantains and cut in half or quarter if you’d like it to cook through faster. Rinse green plantains then add plantains into a large pot with 8 cups of water or until water covers green plantains. Then add 2 tbsp of kosher salt. Note - the water should be slightly salty
  3. Over medium high heat, bring water and green plantains to a boil. Cook plantains until they become soft and a fork can easily pierce through. Once the plantains are cooked, remove plantains and set boiled salted water aside
  4. While the plantains are boiling, saute onions, salt, and vinegar. Be careful not to burn yourself as oil may pop. Once the onions are softened and light pink in color, turn off flame and set aside
  5. In a large mixing bowl while plantains are still hot, add butter, cold water or water from where the plantains were boiling, sauce from the onions and begin to mash. Mash plantains until they are velvety smooth
  6. In the end, add onions and sauce over top. Enjoy with fried Dominican Salami, Fried Dominican Cheese, and a Fried Egg for a traditional Dominican Mangu con Los Tres Golpes. Y ou can also enjoy with other proteins like Ropa Vieja, Shrimp, or Pollo Guisado
